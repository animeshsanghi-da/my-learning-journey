# Business Intelligence: Publishing to Tableau Public

## Overview
Tableau Public is a free platform that allows anyone to explore, create, and publicly share data visualizations online. Publishing your dashboards to Tableau Public is a critical step in building a Data Analytics portfolio, as it provides a live, interactive showcase of your technical skills and data storytelling capabilities for recruiters and peers.

---

## 1. Understanding Tableau Public vs. Tableau Desktop
Before publishing, it is important to understand the constraints and features of Tableau Public:
* **Visibility:** Everything published to Tableau Public is accessible to anyone on the internet. Do not publish sensitive, proprietary, or confidential data (like real client financials or PII).
* **Data Connections:** Tableau Public does not support live connections to local database servers (like a local MySQL server). You must use **Data Extracts** (saving a static snapshot of the data within the workbook) before publishing.
* **Cost:** It is entirely free and hosts your interactive dashboards seamlessly.

---

## 2. Preparing Your Workbook for Publishing
A professional dashboard requires cleanup before it goes live. 

### A. Create a Data Extract
If you connected to a file (like an Excel sheet or a local SQL dump), you must extract the data:
1. Go to the **Data Source** tab in the bottom left corner.
2. In the top right corner, switch the connection type from **Live** to **Extract**.
3. Go to any sheet. Tableau will prompt you to save the `.hyper` extract file to your local machine.

### B. Hide Individual Worksheets
You generally only want users to see the final Dashboard or Story, not the messy individual worksheets used to build it.
1. Right-click on the tabs of your individual worksheets at the bottom of the screen.
2. Select **Hide**. (Note: You can only hide a worksheet if it is currently being used on a Dashboard or Story).

### C. Check Device Layouts
Ensure your dashboard looks good on both desktop and mobile.
1. On your Dashboard tab, click **Device Preview** in the left-hand pane.
2. Select **Phone** to see how the layout stacks. Adjust the layout to "Fit Width" or rearrange tiles so mobile users don't have to scroll horizontally.

---

## 3. The Publishing Process (Step-by-Step)

Follow these steps to push your local workbook to the Tableau Public server:

1. **Sign In:** Go to the top menu bar, click **Server** -> **Tableau Public** -> **Sign In**. Enter your Tableau Public credentials.
2. **Save to Web:** Go to **Server** -> **Tableau Public** -> **Save to Tableau Public As...**
3. **Name Your Workbook:** Give your project a clean, professional title (e.g., `Olist_Ecommerce_Sales_Dashboard`).
4. **Processing:** Tableau will bundle your data extract and visualizations and upload them to the server.
5. **Browser Redirect:** Once successful, your default web browser will open automatically to the live webpage of your newly published dashboard.

---

## 4. Managing and Sharing Your Dashboard

Once your dashboard is live on your Tableau Public profile, you can manage its settings.

### Profile Settings
* **Show/Hide Workbook:** You can toggle an eye icon on your profile to hide workbooks you are still drafting.
* **Allow Downloads:** By default, others can download your workbook to see how you built it. You can disable this in the settings if you only want them to view the interactive web version.

### Embedding in Portfolios and Websites
To share your work on a personal website or an online portfolio (like GitHub Pages or Notion), you can use the embed code provided by Tableau.

1. Click the **Share** icon at the bottom of your live Tableau Public dashboard.
2. Copy the **Embed Code**.
3. Paste the HTML into your website.
